01 · The Story — Problem & what we set out to do

A complex enrollment process causing confusion, calls, and cost

SAG-AFTRA's health plan enrollment process was causing significant confusion for members. The organization needed a modular, scalable solution that could handle diverse eligibility scenarios while improving the user experience and reducing support dependency.

Key insights from discovery revealed three compounding problems: low task completion due to unclear feedback in the multi-step enrollment process; difficulty understanding plan transitions and coverage eligibility; and manual follow-up for payments and deadlines, straining operations.

🔁
Low task completion — unclear feedback in multi-step enrollment
Members abandoned the process mid-flow with no clear indication of what went wrong or what to do next.
🧩
Difficulty understanding plan transitions & coverage eligibility
Members with diverse eligibility scenarios couldn't self-navigate. Every edge case became a call to support.
📞
Manual follow-up for payments and deadlines straining operations
No automated reminders. Staff spent disproportionate time on outreach that should have been self-service.

04 · Retrospect — What went well & what I'd do differently

Key learnings from a regulated, complex domain

What went well
Cross-functional alignment held throughoutGetting design, engineering, and compliance aligned on shared goals at the start meant we didn't re-litigate scope mid-sprint.
CX team as surrogate users was effectiveDespite legal limitations on direct member testing, the CX team's domain knowledge produced high-quality feedback that directly improved the flows.
Modular design unlocked stakeholder confidenceStructuring the MVP as a modular system — rather than a monolithic redesign — made the implementation feel tractable to all parties.
What I'd do differently
Push harder for real member testingThe legal constraints were real, but a limited, anonymized member testing protocol could have been designed. The CX proxy was good — actual member signal would have been better.
Map edge-case eligibility scenarios earlierThe diversity of eligibility configurations surfaced late in the project. A comprehensive scenario mapping exercise in week one would have shaped the information architecture from the start.
Define the AI roadmap in phase oneAI-driven personalization was identified as a future priority — but scoping it earlier would have influenced architectural decisions made in the MVP.
